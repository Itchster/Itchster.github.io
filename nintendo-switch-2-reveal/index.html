Nintendo Switch 2 Reveal

Itchster (Richard Wilson) January 17, 2025 Nintendo

So yesterday, Nintendo released a 2:22 trailer on YouTube for the Nintendo Switch 2, with more information coming on 2/4/2024 (that’s a lot of 2s to be just a coincidence!).

The trailer only really shows off what the hardware looks like and has no info about games, specs/power of the system, or release dates outside of its launching in 2025, which we already knew. But some interesting things were confirmed:

1. The Switch 2 is bigger

It looks like it has an 8-inch screen and thus bigger Joy-Cons as well. I’ll still be putting something like a grip case on it (so comfortable; if you have not tried one yet, do it! It’s a night and day improvement for ergonomics in handheld mode), but a bigger size overall should be more comfortable for me!

2. It has a take on the better hinge of the OLED Switch.

The hinge is the full length of the body, like on the OLED, but thinner. I do wonder how durable this is compared to the OLED, but it should be more stable than the hinge on the original Switch.

3. It has a USB-C port on the top.

It’s next to the headphone jack and the game card slot, should be handy assuming that you can charge from it/use USB-C headphones from it. Could be used for some other accessories Nintendo might have planned as well. There is still a USB-C port on the bottom of the console like the original Switch.

4. It’s backwards compatible with original Switch games, both physical game cards and eShop downloads.

Nintendo does say that it’s not 100% compatible, but if I had to have a guess, these are going to be extreme edge cases, things like the Labo cardboard toys that need you to slot the switch into (hard to do with a physically bigger switch!).

Another possibility is games that use the IR camera on the bottom of the right Joy-Con; it does not look like it’s there on the new Joy-Cons so far, but a workaround for that could be just using original Joy-Cons for those games connected via Bluetooth, but no details yet.

5. The Joy-Cons are magnetic.

 Although it looks smart, and I bet the feel of them clicking into place is satisfying, I do wonder how durable they will be.

6. The Joy-Cons have an optical sensor on them.

Nintendo only briefly showed this sensor off (blink and you miss it), but it’s clearly there, opening up the possibility of using either or both Joy-Cons like a mouse.

So that’s the basics.
